## Executive conclusion

Soheil is strongly positioned for Senior Frontend Engineer, Senior Product
Engineer (Frontend), Frontend Platform Engineer, and founding/sole frontend
roles in product teams building AI, healthcare, B2B SaaS, real-time workflows,
developer tools, or data-heavy internal products.

The main constraint is not skill or time-zone overlap. It is whether an employer
can lawfully engage and pay someone whose actual residence and work location are
in Iran. "Remote," "worldwide," and "EMEA" do not answer that question.

The recommended funnel is therefore:

1. Search remote roles, but run an Iran-residency eligibility gate before doing
   substantial application work or a take-home.
2. Concentrate remote outreach on non-US-controlled GCC, EU, UK, Canadian,
   Australian, and Asian companies, especially founder-led product companies and
   teams that can confirm a lawful direct arrangement.
3. Run a parallel GCC lane focused on Saudi Arabia and the UAE, including remote,
   hybrid, and employer-sponsored relocation.
4. Keep a selective Tehran lane for strong product companies rather than pausing
   the search while waiting for an international path.

## Iran-residency gate

### United States

US-incorporated and US-controlled employers are very-low-probability targets
unless they explicitly confirm that the engagement is authorized. The current
eCFR prohibits importing Iranian-origin services into the US
([31 CFR 560.201](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/part-560/section-560.201))
and defines those services to include work performed in Iran or by a person
residing in Iran
([31 CFR 560.306](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/part-560/section-560.306)).
Rules also restrict US persons' supply of services/technology to Iran and their
facilitation of otherwise prohibited transactions
([31 CFR 560.204](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/part-560/section-560.204),
[31 CFR 560.208](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/part-560/section-560.208)).
Foreign entities owned or controlled by US persons are also covered
([31 CFR 560.215](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-31/part-560/section-560.215)).

### EU and UK

The official EU and UK material describes targeted persons, entities, sectors,
goods, technology, and services rather than a blanket ban on all ordinary work
by every Iran resident. This makes a non-US-controlled employer potentially
possible, but only after sanctions screening, export-control review, and bank
approval. The EU directs operators to national competent authorities for
case-specific guidance
([European Commission contacts](https://finance.ec.europa.eu/eu-and-world/sanctions-restrictive-measures/contacts-eu-sanctions_en));
the UK maintains current Iran sanctions guidance on GOV.UK
([UK guidance](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/iran-sanctions-guidance)).

### Hiring and payment platforms

The standard global hiring stack does not solve this problem. Official sources
currently exclude Iran:

- [Upwork](https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/211067778-Who-s-eligible-to-join-and-use-Upwork)
- [Deel](https://help.letsdeel.com/hc/en-gb/articles/4407737728273-Where-Is-Deel-Available)
- [Remote](https://support.remote.com/hc/en-us/articles/22494726617229-Which-countries-does-Remote-s-Contractor-Management-NOT-cover)
- [Oyster](https://support.oysterhr.com/hc/en-us/articles/4405735992593-Can-I-hire-a-contractor-in-any-country)
- [Plane](https://help.plane.com/en/articles/3672748-pay-list-of-supported-countries)
- [RemotePass](https://help.remotepass.com/en/articles/6088545-unsupported-countries)
- [Wise](https://wise.com/help/articles/2978049/where-can-i-use-wise/yovKZbtbfY.otf)

No mainstream identity-verified payment route supporting an Iran resident was
verified. The employer's legal/compliance team and bank must approve the exact
arrangement. Residence should never be concealed and another person's account,
false address, or undisclosed intermediary should not be used.

### Fast qualification message

> Before I invest in the process, could you confirm whether your legal/payroll

> all work in Tehran, Iran? I work 09:00-20:00 IRST (UTC+03:30), fully covering
> EMEA and GCC hours. If Iran residence is unsupported, I am also open to
> employer-sponsored relocation to the GCC.

A vague answer to either legal engagement or the payment route is a stop signal.

## Recommended operating plan

### Positioning

Use **Senior Frontend Engineer** as the first, searchable identity. Keep
"product-minded" or "Product Curator" as secondary differentiation.

Suggested headline:

> Senior Frontend Engineer | React, Next.js, TypeScript | AI, B2B and real-time
> product workflows | Tehran UTC+03:30 | EMEA/GCC hours

Maintain two truthful application emphases from the same canonical resume:

1. **Remote startup/product:** sole frontend ownership, clinical AI, codebase
   modernization, architecture, real-time reliability, async communication.
2. **GCC enterprise/fintech:** RBAC, dashboards, Nx/shared systems, localization,
   API contracts, performance, supplied-Figma delivery, and enterprise users.

Create three short portfolio case studies: the patient request reduction, live
encounter reliability, and shared Nx/conversational foundations. Each should fit
on one screen and follow problem -> constraints -> decision -> evidence -> result.

### Effort split

Start with:

- **50%** remote-from-Iran leads;
- **35%** GCC remote, hybrid, and sponsored relocation;
- **15%** selective Tehran roles.

If fewer than two employers confirm Iran engagement/payment after three weeks,
shift to 30% remote-from-Iran, 55% GCC, and 15% Tehran while keeping the best
remote alerts active.

### Lead score

Score every role before applying:

| Dimension | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Lawful Iran engagement/payment or explicit GCC sponsorship | 25 |
| React/Next.js/TypeScript and architecture fit | 20 |
| Product/domain fit | 15 |
| Senior ownership without excessive management expectations | 15 |
| Tehran-hours overlap | 15 |
| Compensation/stability | 10 |

Do not proceed if the first dimension is zero. Otherwise prioritize scores of
75 or higher.

### Weekly cadence

- Source 20-25 leads and qualify residency/location before deep tailoring.
- Submit 8-12 high-fit applications, ideally within 72 hours of posting.
- Send 15 focused messages to founders, engineering leaders, employees, and MENA
  recruiters; ask for a short fit/eligibility conversation, not just a referral.
- Hold two networking conversations with former colleagues, diaspora founders,
  or engineers already working remotely across the GCC/EMEA corridor.
- Follow up once after five business days, then close the loop.
- Review the funnel every Friday: qualified leads, eligibility confirmations,
  recruiter screens, technical interviews, finals, and reasons for rejection.

After 20 qualified applications, diagnose by stage: under 10% recruiter-screen
conversion suggests positioning/targeting needs work; screens but no technical
rounds suggest the story or eligibility explanation needs work; technical rounds
without finals suggest interview practice should become the priority.
